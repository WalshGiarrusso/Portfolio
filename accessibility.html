Accessibility Statement

Accessibility is this website's top priority.

I am committed to making this website as close to perfectly accessible as I possibly can. It's not because the people who visit this website often have disabilites. It's because I believe in both the value and feasibility of a truly accessible internet. In theory, the internet should be incredible for people with disabilities. Someone who is paralyzed on their left side can scroll, tap, and swipe as good as anyone else. Someone who is blind can turn any book into an audiobook using a screen reader. However, in reality, research shows that people with disabilites are less likely to use the internet, not more. Why? Because, almost everything else in their life, it wasn't designed with them in mind.

If I had to sum up my life purpose in one sentence, I would say that my purpose is to be the person who cares about someone when other people don't. People with disabilites are some of people our society forgets to consider the most often, especially when it's easier to say "who cares?" I think making the web accessible to the people who would benefit the most from it isn't some idealistic fantasy; I think it's common courtesy.
