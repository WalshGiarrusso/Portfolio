The internet is humanity's greatest achievement.
I believe everybody has a right to share in its benefits.

The internet has enabled me to better myself my entire life.

I've used the internet to teach myself everything from coding to graphic design, and that ability to self-teach has itself become a major life skill. It would be hard to overstate the positive impact the internet has had on my life. It has been transformative, both for me as a person and for my self-confidence.

Now, I'm returning the favor by designing smarter, more accessible websites.

The internet's positive impact on my life led me to the same conclusion as the United Nations: that access to the internet is a human right. Using the self-starting attitude instilled in me by the internet, I began to wonder what I could do as an individual to improve people's access to the internet and its benefits. While it's true that developing countries contain the largest concentrations of people without internet access, it's very unlikely that I could make an impact on that front as an individual. I decided that I could make an impact by trying to make the internet more accessible for people with disabilities. In order improve internet accessibility for people with disabilities, I am now actively engaged in promoting and practicing accessible web design.
